A film guide designed for the Sundance Film Festival, 2016 issue. This design brief asked students to construct and design an A5 zine publication based upon a festival or conference event. To create a level of individuality, the students needed to employ the use of analog techniques appropriate to the content and concept of the festival event.

With a great love of independent film, I quickly chose the Sundance Film Festival. Breaking down the semiotic layers of film, I reimagined film stills in the form of individual cel-animation techniques. Instead of separating film by way of motion, I separated thumbnail, costume, character and setting to show how one image has many layers to create intended meaning.

Using a simple colour scheme, carefully chosen typefaces and carefully layered tracing paper, acting as cel-animation sheets, all bound within an A5 saddle-stitched brochure intended for all to peruse.
