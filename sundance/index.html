This design brief asked students to construct and design an A5 zine publication based upon a festival or any conference event that may find of interest. To create a level of individuality, the students needed to employ the use of analog techniques appropriate to the concept, and content of the festival event.

With a great love of independent film, I quickly chose the Sundance Film Festival. I broke down the semiotic layers of film and reimagined a series of film stills in the form of individual cel-animation layers. Instead of separating film by way of motion, I separated thumbnail, costume, character and setting to show how one image has many layers to create the intended meaning.

I used a simple colour scheme, carefully chosen typefaces and layered tracing paper, all bound within an A5 saddle-stitched brochure intended for all to peruse.
