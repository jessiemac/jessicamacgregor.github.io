This design brief focussed on a specific location within Melbourne, a location which the designer must interact to generate a collection of assets specific to that location.
The assets, or material generated may be from a variety of media ranging from photography, to textural found fabrics.

I quickly chose a laneway in Melbourne's CBD - an area rich in inspiration in between Bourke and Little Bourke Street. The laneway provides access to architectural businesses, apartment blocks and the now closed Palace Theatre.

I found discarded fabrics, tiles and architectural sketches.
I sketched contour drawings and took daily photography to manipulate and properly reflect the public space. I bound the final document with a wire spiral in the shape of half an A4 page, cut lengthways, to mimick the shape of the laneway itself. The publication is reflexive, and can be opened at any intersection point.
